Today, I want to talk about something simple, yet powerful: progress. Not perfection, not overnight success, but progress—the small steps we take every single day that shape who we become.

We often live in a world that celebrates big achievements. We see the end results: the awards, the promotions, the victories. But what we don’t always see are the countless hours of effort, the failures, and the moments of doubt that came before them. Progress lives in those unseen moments.

Think about it. Every skill you have today started with a first attempt. Maybe it wasn’t good. Maybe it was uncomfortable. But you kept going. That’s progress.

The truth is, you don’t need to have everything figured out to move forward. You just need to start. One step. One decision. One action. Over time, those small steps build momentum, and momentum creates change.

There will be days when progress feels slow. Days when you question whether it’s worth it. In those moments, remember this: growth is not always visible, but it is always happening when you keep trying.

So don’t be discouraged by how far you have to go. Instead, focus on how far you’ve already come. Celebrate the small wins. Learn from the setbacks. And most importantly, keep moving forward.

Because in the end, success is not about being perfect. It’s about being persistent.

Thank you.
